# The Pre-Read: Your Most Underrated Leadership Tool

Most engineers think the meeting is where decisions happen. They're wrong. The decision already happened before anyone sat down. It happened when someone read the pre-read.

## Why pre-reads matter more than you think

At the Staff+ level, you're no longer evaluated on how well you present in a room. You're evaluated on how well you set up the room before anyone walks in. The pre-read is your single best tool for this.

A good pre-read does three things:

- It aligns everyone on the same facts before the meeting starts
- It surfaces disagreements early so you can address them 1:1
- It makes the actual meeting shorter and more focused on decisions

## The anatomy of a good pre-read

Keep it to one page. Seriously. If your pre-read is five pages, nobody is reading it. They'll show up to the meeting having skimmed the first paragraph and you'll spend 20 minutes getting everyone to the same baseline.

Structure it like this:

**Context**: One paragraph. What's the situation, why are we here.

**Proposal**: What you're recommending and why. Be specific.

**Open questions**: 2-3 things you actually need input on. Not rhetorical questions. Real ones where you genuinely don't know the answer.

**Ask**: What decision do you need from this meeting.

## Common mistakes

The biggest mistake I see is treating the pre-read as a status update. Nobody needs a meeting to read a status update. The pre-read should be opinionated. Take a position. If you're not willing to be wrong in the pre-read, you're not being useful.

The second mistake is sending it too late. If you send the pre-read 30 minutes before the meeting, you might as well not send it at all. Two business days is the sweet spot.

## The meta-skill

Writing good pre-reads is really about developing a communication habit that scales. As you get more senior, you can't be in every room. Your documents travel where you can't. Making those documents clear, opinionated, and actionable is one of the highest-leverage skills you can build.
